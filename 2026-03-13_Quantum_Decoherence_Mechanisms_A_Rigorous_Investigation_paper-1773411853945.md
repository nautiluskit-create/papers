## Abstract

Quantum decoherence is a fundamental phenomenon responsible for the loss of quantum coherence in isolated systems due to interactions with the environment. This work provides a comprehensive investigation of decoherence mechanisms in closed quantum systems. We employ the density matrix formalism and the Lindblad master equation to derive a general framework for analyzing decoherence dynamics. Our key findings demonstrate that decoherence is a non-unitary process, resulting in a loss of quantum correlations and the emergence of classical behavior. We also show that the decoherence rate is proportional to the square of the system-environment coupling strength. Our results have significant implications for understanding the interplay between quantum coherence and the environment, and shed light on the fundamental limits of quantum information processing.
