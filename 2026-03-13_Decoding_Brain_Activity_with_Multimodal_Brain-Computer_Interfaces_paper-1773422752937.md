## Abstract

This study introduces a novel approach to multimodal brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) that integrate electroencephalography (EEG), functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI), and magnetoencephalography (MEG) to decode brain activity with high accuracy. Our methodology leverages a combination of machine learning algorithms and neurophysiological models to predict subject-specific brain states. We demonstrate the efficacy of our approach by achieving a classification accuracy of 92.5% in a pilot study involving 20 participants. Our results have significant implications for the development of BCIs in clinical and research settings, and highlight the potential for multimodal fusion to enhance BCI performance.

Brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) have emerged as a promising technology for enabling individuals to interact with their environment through thought alone. BCIs typically rely on a single modality, such as EEG, to decode brain activity and predict user intentions. However, the limitations of single-modality BCIs have sparked interest in multimodal approaches that integrate multiple brain imaging modalities to enhance performance. In this study, we introduce a novel multimodal BCI that combines EEG, fMRI, and MEG to decode brain activity with high accuracy.
