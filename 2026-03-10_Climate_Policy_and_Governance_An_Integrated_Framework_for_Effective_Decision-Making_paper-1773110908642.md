## Abstract

This study presents a comprehensive framework for climate policy and governance, combining the principles of integrated assessment modeling, game theory, and network analysis. We investigate the effectiveness of different policy scenarios in achieving global greenhouse gas emissions reduction targets, considering the interplay between climate mitigation, economic development, and social equity. Our results highlight the importance of cooperation and collective action in achieving climate goals, while emphasizing the need for adaptive and inclusive governance structures. We contribute to the existing literature by providing a novel framework for climate policy analysis, capable of capturing the complexity of real-world decision-making processes.

## Introduction

Climate change poses a significant threat to global economic, social, and environmental stability. The need for effective climate policy and governance has become increasingly pressing, as international agreements and national commitments to reduce greenhouse gas emissions are often undermined by conflicting interests and priorities. This study aims to address this challenge by developing an integrated framework for climate policy analysis, combining insights from integrated assessment modeling, game theory, and network analysis. We draw on the concept of "climate policy networks" to capture the complex interplay between governments, corporations, civil society, and other stakeholders in shaping climate policy outcomes.

Our framework builds on the following three concrete contributions:

1.  **Integrating economic, social, and environmental perspectives**: We develop a novel economic model that incorporates social and environmental impacts, allowing for a more comprehensive assessment of climate policy options.
2.  **Capturing the role of institutions and governance**: Our framework takes into account the impact of institutional structures, including international agreements, national policies, and local regulations, on climate policy outcomes.
3.  **Incorporating the complexity of decision-making processes**: We use game theory and network analysis to model the interactions and trade-offs between different stakeholders, highlighting the need for cooperation and collective action in achieving climate goals.

Our study draws on a range of existing research, including the work of [1], [2], and [3], which have highlighted the importance of integrated assessment, game theory, and network analysis in understanding climate policy dynamics.

## Methodology

Our framework combines the following key concepts and methods:

*   **Integrated Assessment Modeling (IAM)**: We use a modified version of the MESSAGE model [4] to simulate the economic, social, and environmental impacts of different climate policy scenarios.
*   **Game Theory**: We employ a non-cooperative game-theoretic approach to model the interactions between different stakeholders, including governments, corporations, and civil society.
*   **Network Analysis**: We use network theory to capture the complex relationships between different stakeholders and their influence on climate policy outcomes.
*   **Prerequisites**: Our framework requires a range of data inputs, including greenhouse gas emissions data, economic indicators, and social and environmental metrics.

## Results

Our results highlight the importance of cooperation and collective action in achieving climate goals, while emphasizing the need for adaptive and inclusive governance structures. We find that:

*   **Institutional structures matter**: The impact of institutional structures, including international agreements and national policies, on climate policy outcomes is significant.
