## Abstract

This study investigates the relationship between neuromodulation and brain plasticity through a computational modeling approach, focusing on synaptic homeostasis and neural dynamics. We develop a novel network model of the neocortex based on the spike-phase-dependent synaptic plasticity (SPSP) rule, which incorporates the effects of neuromodulatory inputs on synaptic strength and neural excitability. Using a combination of numerical simulations and analytical techniques, we demonstrate that the SPSP rule can generate realistic patterns of neural activity and synaptic reorganization, consistent with empirical findings from brain development and neuroplasticity research. Our results suggest that neuromodulation plays a crucial role in regulating synaptic homeostasis and neural adaptation, with implications for understanding neurological and psychiatric disorders.
