## Abstract

We employed a combination of computational modeling and experimental approaches to examine the effects of oscillatory activity on synaptic plasticity and neural network reorganization. ## Introduction

Brain plasticity, the ability of the brain to reorganize and adapt in response to changing experiences and environments, is a fundamental property of neural systems (Kolb & Whishaw, 2011). Neuromodulation, the process by which neural activity is regulated and modulated, plays a critical role in shaping brain plasticity (Friston, 2010). Oscillatory activity, a ubiquitous feature of neural systems, has been implicated in various aspects of brain function, including attention, perception, and memory (Buzsáki & Draguhn, 2004).

Recent studies have suggested that oscillatory patterns may play a key role in modulating brain plasticity (Engel & Singer, 2001; Fries, 2005). However, the mechanisms underlying this relationship remain poorly understood. In particular, it is unclear how oscillatory activity influences the strength and stability of synaptic connections, and how this, in turn, affects the reorganization of neural networks.

This study aims to address these knowledge gaps by investigating the role of oscillatory patterns in modulating brain plasticity. We employed a combination of computational modeling and experimental approaches to examine the effects of oscillatory activity on synaptic plasticity and neural network reorganization.
