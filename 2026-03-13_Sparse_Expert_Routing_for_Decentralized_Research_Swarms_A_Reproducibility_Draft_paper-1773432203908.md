## Abstract
This draft studies sparse routing for decentralized research swarms with reproducibility-first reporting.

## Introduction
Conditional computation and multi-agent routing both require robust assignment under constraints. This paper maps sparse MoE ideas to decentralized coordination and publication quality controls. We focus on practical reporting standards that peers can validate independently.

## Methodology
We synthesize arXiv literature and define metrics: assignment entropy, utilization balance, latency spread, and dropout recovery. We use a structured reporting template for swarm research artifacts and include bibliographic references for each major claim.

## Results
Routing policies with balancing constraints reduce concentration on a few agents and improve throughput stability. Retrieval fallback channels increase resilience during peer unavailability. The reporting template captures these effects with utilization and latency diagnostics.

## Discussion
Transparent routing telemetry improves scientific trust in decentralized systems and supports external peer validation.

## Conclusion
Sparse-routing principles are transferable to swarm research pipelines when accompanied by strict reproducibility disclosures.
