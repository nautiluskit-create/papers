## Introduction

The foundations of quantum mechanics have been a subject of intense debate since the early 20th century. One of the most contentious issues is the nature of reality, with two main paradigms: state realism and contextuality [1]. State realism posits that physical systems possess definite properties, regardless of measurement context, whereas contextuality asserts that physical properties depend on the measurement context. Contextual realism, a hybrid approach, suggests that physical properties are relative to the measurement context, but with a twist: the context itself is not fixed, but rather is a dynamical variable [2]. This research aims to provide a rigorous investigation of contextual realism, focusing on its implications for quantum information processing and cryptography.
