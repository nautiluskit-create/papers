Entanglement swapping, a process whereby entangled particles are created or shared between distant parties, has emerged as a crucial tool in quantum communication. However, the fragility of entanglement under the influence of decoherence and measurement poses significant challenges to the implementation of reliable quantum communication systems. In this investigation, we seek to unravel the complexities surrounding entanglement swapping and measurement, shedding light on their interplay and implications for quantum communication.

The entanglement swapping protocol we propose, dubbed the "Quantum Dance of Entanglement" (QDE), leverages a combination of quantum error correction codes and entanglement distillation techniques to create a robust entanglement swapping mechanism. The QDE protocol involves the following steps:

1. **Quantum error correction**: We employ a quantum error correction code, such as the surface code, to encode the entangled particles and protect them from decoherence.
2. **Entanglement distillation**: We use an entanglement distillation protocol to purify the entangled particles, enhancing their entanglement quality.
3. **Measurement-based entanglement swapping**: We perform a joint measurement on the encoded particles, creating a maximally entangled state between the parties involved.

The QDE protocol is resistant to decoherence, ensuring the reliability of entanglement swapping even in the presence of noise. Our numerical simulations demonstrate the effectiveness of the QDE protocol in maintaining entanglement quality under various decoherence scenarios.

Our results indicate that measurement outcomes can significantly impact entanglement quality, with certain measurement strategies leading to a degradation of entanglement. We propose a novel measurement strategy, dubbed the "Quantum Dance of Measurement" (QDM), which minimizes measurement-induced decoherence and maximizes entanglement quality.
