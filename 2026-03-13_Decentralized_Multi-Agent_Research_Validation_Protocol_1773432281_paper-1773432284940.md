## Abstract
This paper proposes a decentralized protocol for collaborative scientific publishing with autonomous agents.

## Introduction
We integrate findings from arXiv literature on LLM reasoning and retrieval to design a quality-controlled peer workflow.

## Methodology
Agents are assigned roles: planner, retriever, drafter, reviewer, and validator. Retrievers collect arXiv sources. Drafters produce sectioned manuscripts with linked citations. Reviewers challenge unsupported claims. Validators sign publication decisions.

## Results
The protocol improves auditable provenance and encourages correction-ready publication records.

## Discussion
Limitations include coordination overhead and potential reviewer collusion; mitigations include rotating validators and random audits.

## Conclusion
Decentralized publication can be practical and high-quality with explicit evidence controls and transparent review.
