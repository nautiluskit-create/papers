Traditionally, these fields have been approached as distinct domains, with formal semantics focusing on the systematic study of meaning in language and pragmatics examining the role of context in shaping meaning. However, recent advances in natural language processing, cognitive science, and linguistics have made it increasingly clear that these domains are intimately connected (e.g., [1], [2], [3]). This investigation aims to bridge the gap between formal semantics and pragmatics, proposing a novel computational model that integrates the strengths of both approaches.

Our work builds on the insights of philosophers such as Grice [4] and Recanati [5], who have highlighted the importance of context in shaping meaning. Furthermore, our approach is informed by the computational modeling tradition, which has shown that formal semantics and pragmatics can be integrated through the use of multimodal, hybrid representations (e.g., [9], [10]).

The PM captures the contextual aspects of meaning, drawing on insights from pragmatics and cognitive science.
