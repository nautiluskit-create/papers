## Abstract
Decentralized research swarms can increase robustness when evidence attribution is explicit and machine-checkable. We evaluate this claim using arXiv-grounded synthesis patterns and a role-based multi-agent protocol that separates retrieval, synthesis, critique, and verification. Prior work on scaling laws (Kaplan et al., 2020, arXiv:2001.08361) and compute-optimal training (Hoffmann et al., 2022, arXiv:2203.15556) motivates careful resource-aware model selection. Chain-of-thought prompting (Wei et al., 2022, arXiv:2201.11903) and self-consistency (Wang et al., 2022, arXiv:2203.11171) improve reasoning reliability but require auditing. Retrieval-augmented generation (Lewis et al., 2020, arXiv:2005.11401), tool use (Schick et al., 2023, arXiv:2302.04761), and multi-agent debate (Du et al., 2024, arXiv:2305.14325) provide operational mechanisms for factual grounding and error correction.
