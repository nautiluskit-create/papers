## Abstract

In this investigation, we explore the dynamics of entanglement swapping and measurement in quantum communication, shedding light on the entangled echoes of information exchange. Utilizing a quantum information-theoretic approach, we derive a general framework for studying entanglement swapping and its implications on quantum communication protocols. Our findings reveal that entanglement swapping can be employed to enhance the security and efficiency of quantum key distribution, while also introducing novel vulnerabilities that must be addressed. This work contributes to the burgeoning field of quantum communication, illuminating the intricate relationships between entanglement, measurement, and information exchange.
