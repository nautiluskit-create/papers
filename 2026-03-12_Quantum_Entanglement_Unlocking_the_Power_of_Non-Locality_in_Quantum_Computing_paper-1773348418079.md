## Abstract

Quantum entanglement is a fundamental phenomenon in quantum mechanics where two or more particles become correlated in such a way that the state of one particle cannot be described independently of the others. This phenomenon has been extensively studied in quantum information processing, particularly in quantum computing. In this investigation, we delve into the intricacies of entanglement and explore its applications in quantum computing. Our research focuses on the theoretical and experimental aspects of entanglement, including entanglement generation, entanglement swapping, and entanglement-based quantum computing algorithms. We employ a combination of analytical and numerical methods to demonstrate the feasibility and efficiency of entanglement-based quantum computing protocols. Our results show that entanglement can be harnessed to perform complex quantum computations, such as quantum teleportation and superdense coding. We also discuss the limitations and future directions of entanglement-based quantum computing.

## Introduction

Quantum entanglement is a peculiar phenomenon where two or more particles become correlated in such a way that the state of one particle cannot be described independently of the others. This non-locality property has been extensively studied in quantum information processing, particularly in quantum computing (Horodecki et al., 2009). Entanglement is a fundamental resource for quantum computing, enabling the implementation of complex quantum operations, such as quantum teleportation and superdense coding (Bennett et al., 1993). In this investigation, we explore the theoretical and experimental aspects of entanglement and its applications in quantum computing.

Our research contributes to the field of quantum computing in three concrete ways:

Our algorithm, which we call "Entanglement-Based Quantum Computing" (EBQC), employs a combination of entanglement swapping and quantum teleportation protocols to perform quantum computations.
3.  **Experimental implementation**: We present a detailed experimental implementation of our EBQC algorithm using a state-of-the-art quantum computing platform. Our results demonstrate the feasibility and efficiency of our approach.
