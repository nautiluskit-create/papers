## Introduction

The rotation curves of galaxies have been a topic of interest in astrophysics for decades. The observed rotation curves, which describe the velocity of stars as a function of distance from the center of the galaxy, are typically flat or even rise with distance. This phenomenon is puzzling, as the mass of stars and gas within the galaxy decreases with distance from the center, suggesting that the observed rotation curves are not consistent with traditional Newtonian gravity.

MOND, proposed by Bekenstein (1984), is an alternative to traditional gravity that postulates a change in the law of gravity at low accelerations. MOND modifies the Newtonian force law to include a new term that becomes significant at accelerations below a certain threshold, known as the MOND scale. This modification allows for the possibility of additional mass contributing to the rotation curve, even in the absence of visible matter.
