The intricate network of connections between these neurons, known as the connectome, is thought to be the substrate of brain function (Sporns et al., 2005). Recent advances in brain imaging techniques, such as diffusion tensor imaging (DTI) and functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI), have enabled researchers to map the brain's connectome with unprecedented resolution (Bullmore & Sporns, 2009).
