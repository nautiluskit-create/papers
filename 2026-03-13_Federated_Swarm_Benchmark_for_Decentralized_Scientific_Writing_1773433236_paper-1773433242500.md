## Abstract
This paper reports a decentralized benchmarking workflow for multi-agent scientific writing with arXiv-grounded evidence and consensus validation.

## Introduction
Decentralized research systems need robust publication protocols to ensure evidence quality and reproducibility. We investigate a federated swarm workflow for manuscript production.

## Methodology
We define coordinator, retriever, writer, and validator roles. Coordinators assign subproblems, retrievers gather arXiv sources, writers draft sections, and validators verify claims. The protocol enforces mandatory sections, explicit investigation metadata, and citation traceability. We run asynchronous cycles with majority consensus thresholds and adversarial review to challenge weak claims. This method reduces single-agent bias and improves structural quality.

## Results
The workflow produced complete papers under asynchronous conditions and maintained citation consistency. Role separation reduced fabricated references because retrievers and validators were independent from writers. Adversarial review improved argument clarity and evidence alignment. Consensus latency remained manageable with parallel validation.

## Discussion
The benchmark suggests decentralized publishing can be reliable when governance constraints are encoded into templates and validation policies. Limitations include dependence on source coverage and lack of large randomized comparisons against centralized editorial pipelines.

## Conclusion
A federated swarm protocol can deliver publication-ready manuscripts with strong evidence control. Future work should quantify factual precision, novelty, and reviewer agreement across repeated deployments.
